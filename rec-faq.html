Recreational Soccer FAQ

Frequently asked questions about our recreational youth soccer program

Youth Soccer Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Geneva's Recreational Soccer program. If you don't see your question answered here, please contact us.

What equipment will I need to buy?

The only equipment that is required are shin guards and water bottle. Most children like to bring their own ball to practice, and we encourage them to do so.

What size ball do I need?

For practice, children can use any size ball. For games:

Under 6: Size 3

Under 10 and Under 8: Size 4

Under 14 and Under 12: Size 5

When are practices?

Teams practice 1-2 times per week at a time set by the coach. Generally practices begin at 5, 6 or 7 p.m. on weeknights. If you would like to set the practice schedule, we encourage you to volunteer to coach!

When are games?

Games are typically Saturday mornings, but there are typically 1-2 weekday games.

When is the Spring 2025 Season?

Our young athletes will compete with and against similar aged community members over a 9-game season that starts in May.

Do you need volunteers to help coach?

We can always use volunteers! When you register your child, please sign yourself up to volunteer if you are interested and able.

Are there requirements to coach?

All coaches will undergo a background check and be asked to complete an online safety training course that takes ~1 hour to complete.

What should I expect as a coach?

You will be expected to schedule and lead 2 practices a week. As coach, you can set the schedule.

You will receive a team roster and need to call all parents of your players.

On game days, you will coach the team, do substitutions and ensure kids have fun and abide by the rules.

Is there any help available for coaches?

We will conduct a coaches training at the beginning of the season to show a few age-appropriate drills to best teach fundamentals and conduct effective practices.

In addition, our soccer board has many experienced rec and travel coaches who would be happy to offer advice and guidance.

What age groups are available?

Our recreational program typically serves players ages 4-13, though divisions may vary by season depending on registration numbers. All skill levels are welcome, and we focus on fun and fundamental skill development.

Is recreational soccer competitive?

Our recreational program emphasizes fun, skill development, and equal playing time rather than competition. While we keep score during games, the focus is on learning and enjoying the sport in a supportive environment.

How are teams formed?

Teams are typically formed based on age groups and balanced to ensure fair play. We aim to create teams that allow all players to participate equally and develop their skills throughout the season.

What if my child has never played soccer before?

Recreational soccer is perfect for beginners! Our program is designed to teach basic soccer skills and rules in a fun, non-intimidating environment. No prior experience is necessary.

Are there makeup games for weather cancellations?

Games cancelled due to weather conditions may be rescheduled when possible. However, due to field availability and scheduling constraints, not all cancelled games can be made up.

Can parents help during practices and games?

Parent involvement is always welcome! You can help by volunteering as an assistant coach, helping with team management, or simply cheering on all the players during games.

What if I have more questions?
