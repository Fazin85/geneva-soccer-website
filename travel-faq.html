Travel Soccer Questions & Answers

Find answers to common questions about Geneva's Travel Soccer program. If you don't see your question answered here, please contact us.

What is travel soccer?

Geneva Travel Soccer is a program designed to develop youth players beyond what is typically learned in Rec soccer. Geneva currently has several teams in many different age groups. These teams play against teams from nearby communities in Northeast Ohio. Typically, teams play one game per week and practice twice weekly during the season.

What age do children start playing travel soccer?

Children usually start in second grade depending on their date of birth. However, the program is always looking for interested players at almost every age group. A travel soccer player will typically play with the same team throughout their travel career resulting in the player forming bonds with teammates and making strong friendships.

How much travel is involved?

The travel season is run very much like the Rec league. Teams play a spring and fall session. Each session is comprised of four home games and four away games. Away games are played at nearby communities, generally within one hour travel time. Travel soccer games are usually played on Sunday afternoons.

Who coaches the teams?

Coaches are selected and overseen by the Geneva Soccer board. Coaches are usually parents with prior soccer/coaching experience. All coaches must possess a coaching license from the United States Youth Soccer Association which is issued only after the candidate completes the mandated hours of classroom and on-the-field training. All coaches must also complete a background check.

How do I get on a travel team?

At the conclusion of our spring rec program we hold a travel soccer tryout for the following fall travel session. Any players interested in joining a travel team will get a chance to meet the coaches and showcase their soccer skills. All the players that try out will be referred to a coach based on age and gender. The coach will then contact you. We do not hold a formal tryout for players looking to join the spring session but we can get you in contact with the appropriate coach and you can arrange a tryout.

What is the time commitment for travel soccer?

Travel soccer requires a higher level of commitment than recreational soccer. Players typically practice twice per week and have one game per week during the season. Games are usually on Sunday afternoons, and practices are scheduled based on field availability and coach schedules.

What equipment is needed for travel soccer?

Players need soccer cleats, shin guards, a soccer ball (size varies by age group), and a water bottle. The team will provide uniforms including jersey, shorts, and socks. Additional training gear may be recommended by coaches.

What are the costs associated with travel soccer?

Registration fees cover league fees, referees, field usage, and uniforms. Additional costs may include tournament fees, travel expenses for away games, and optional team activities. Specific costs are provided during registration.

Can my child play both rec and travel soccer?

Due to the time commitment and scheduling conflicts, players typically choose either recreational or travel soccer for each season. We recommend discussing with coaches to determine the best fit for your child's skill level and availability.

What if my child is new to competitive soccer?

We welcome players at all skill levels who are interested in competitive play. Coaches will work with players to develop their skills throughout the season. Some players benefit from starting in recreational soccer before moving to travel.

How are teams formed?

Teams are typically formed based on age groups and skill level assessments. We aim to create balanced teams that allow all players to develop and contribute. Team rosters may vary in size depending on the number of registered players.

What leagues do Geneva travel teams participate in?

Geneva travel teams compete in regional leagues against other communities in Northeast Ohio. The specific league may vary by age group and season based on team strength and availability.

Are there tournaments in addition to regular games?

Some teams may choose to participate in tournaments throughout the season. Tournament participation is typically optional and involves additional fees. Coaches will communicate tournament opportunities with parents.

What happens if the weather is bad?

Games and practices may be cancelled or postponed due to severe weather conditions, including lightning, heavy rain, or extreme temperatures. Coaches and league officials will communicate any schedule changes as soon as possible.

How can parents get involved?

Parents are encouraged to support their children and teams in various ways, including volunteering as team managers, helping with transportation to away games, and supporting fundraising activities. Coaching opportunities are also available for qualified parents.

Still Have Questions?
